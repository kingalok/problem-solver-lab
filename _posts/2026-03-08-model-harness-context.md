Everyone is still comparing models.

But the real AI war has already moved up the stack.

For the last two years, most of the attention sat in the model layer:

- GPT vs Claude vs Gemini
- context windows
- benchmarks
- open vs closed
- DeepSeek vs frontier labs

That mattered.

It is just no longer the whole story.

## The next layer is the harness

The model is the intelligence.

The harness is how that intelligence actually operates inside work:

- where it runs
- what it can touch
- what it remembers
- how it verifies
- how much autonomy it gets
- how safe it is inside an enterprise

That is the layer many people still skip, and it is where the philosophies are now diverging.

![AI stack layers visual]({{ '/assets/images/AI-Stack.png' | relative_url }})

## Three different directions already visible

Copilot looks like the enterprise-native harness: grounded in Microsoft Graph, wrapped in permissions, governance, and enterprise controls.

Codex looks more like an AI contractor in a controlled room: sandboxed, isolated, and safe by design.

Claude Code and Cowork push in a different direction: AI as a collaborator inside the workspace itself.

So the main design trade-off is no longer just model quality.

It is increasingly about:

- control vs capability
- governance vs flexibility
- top-down enterprise adoption vs bottom-up builder adoption

![AI harness comparison visual]({{ '/assets/images/Compare-AI-Harnesses.png' | relative_url }})

## The battle after harness is context

Even that may not be the end state.

The next battle is context.

Today, company knowledge is scattered across:

- GitHub
- Jira
- Slack
- Confluence
- Salesforce
- SharePoint
- people's heads

Humans are still the synthesis layer connecting all of it.

That is why losing a senior developer is always a manifold loss.

Not just code, but:

- architecture decisions
- debugging intuition
- undocumented trade-offs
- operational memory
- the mental map of how systems really work

## What AI has the chance to capture

AI's real opportunity is to capture and operationalise that organisational context.

The company that solves context engineering at enterprise scale does not just build a better assistant.

It becomes the system that understands how the organisation thinks.

## My current framing

The AI stack is shifting in three layers:

## Model -> Harness -> Context

Models started the AI race.

Harness determines how AI works.

Context may decide who wins.
